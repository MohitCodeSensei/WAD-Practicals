SpaceX

Space Exploration Technologies Corp. — aerospace manufacturer and space transport services company focused on lowering space access cost and enabling Mars colonization.

Founded: 2002 · Founder: Elon Musk · Headquarters: Hawthorne, California

Overview

SpaceX designs, manufactures and launches advanced rockets and spacecraft. Primary products include the Falcon 9 and Falcon Heavy launch vehicles, Dragon spacecraft family, Starlink satellite constellation and Starship — a fully reusable spacecraft system being developed for deep-space missions.

Major programs

Falcon 9

Two-stage orbital launch vehicle with a reusable first stage. Used for commercial launches, ISS resupply, crew transport (with Crew Dragon) and Starlink deployment.

Falcon Heavy

Heavy-lift variant using three Falcon cores. Offers higher payload capacity for heavy payloads and interplanetary missions.

Dragon & Crew Dragon

Cargo Dragon transports supplies to the ISS. Crew Dragon (Dragon 2) transports astronauts — first private vehicle to carry humans to the ISS.

Starship

Next-generation fully reusable spacecraft and super heavy booster intended for Mars missions, lunar landings (NASA Human Landing System selected Starship for Artemis), and high-capacity Earth-to-Earth transport.

Starlink

Large satellite constellation providing global broadband internet. Important revenue stream and operational system for space-to-ground connectivity.

Quick facts

  • First orbital launch: Falcon 1 (2008)
  • First successful first-stage landing: 2015
  • First commercial crewed flight to ISS: 2020 (Crew Dragon Demo-2)
  • Starlink beta active in many countries

Careers & culture

Engineers cite fast iteration, hardware + software integration, hands-on manufacturing and mission-driven teams. Hiring focuses on spacecraft, propulsion, avionics, software, production and operations.

Careers @ SpaceX

Technology & engineering highlights

  • Reusable rocketry: rapid turnaround and booster landings reduce launch cost.
  • Merlin & Raptor engines: Merlin family powers Falcon series (kerosene/LOX). Raptor (methane/LOX) designed for Starship high-performance cycles.
  • Autonomous flight software: real-time guidance, navigation and control, and precision landing algorithms.
  • Vertical integration: in-house manufacturing for avionics, tanks, structures and software to accelerate iteration.

Operational footprint

Launch sites include Cape Canaveral (LC-40), Kennedy Space Center (LC-39A), Vandenberg Space Force Base, and Boca Chica (Starbase) for Starship testing. Manufacturing & R&D centered in Hawthorne, with additional facilities for production and testing across the U.S.

Recent achievements & milestones

  • Routine Falcon 9 re-flights enabling commercial launch cadence.
  • Successful Crew Dragon crewed missions to the ISS for NASA.
  • Starlink rapidly deployed thousands of satellites and broadened global coverage.
  • Continued Starship prototype development with high-altitude testing and iterative improvements.

Recommended prep: systems-level projects, embedded/real-time software, control systems, propulsion fundamentals, CAD & manufacturing skills, and internships. Contribute to open-source projects and build demonstrable hardware/software projects to stand out.
